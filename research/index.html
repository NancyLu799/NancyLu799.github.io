Research Papers and Publications

Model to Predict Alzheimer's Disease

This is my final project for CBB 452: Biomedical Data Science (Mining and Modeling). The goal of this project was to calculate the Alzheimer's Disease risk of a patient, given his genome. Given the high heritability of Alzheimer's Disease as well as its late onset, studying this disease has immense potential in genomics. We used SNP annotation tools alongside GWAS studies in order to acccomplish this task.

Analyzing Effects of Mental Health in Bariatric Surgery Patients

Since the summer of 2016, I have been working at the Magee-Womens Research Center (University of Pittsburgh Medical Center) on analyzing outcomes of bariatric surgery, and how these outcomes relate to mental health factors. Through statistical analyses including repeated-measured ANCOVA analyses and interaction analysis, I studied a large cohort of bariatric surgery outcomes and found that former physical, mental, or sexual abuse played a significant role in surgical outcomes. Paper to be published in late 2018.

Creating Validated Mental Health Scales for Children

To help answer the question when children should seek help for mental health issues, I helped create 2 validated psychological scales through principle components analysis of results from extensive focus groups and prior research. These scales are targeted to parents and guardians to determine the best time to seek treatment for children's mental health. This research was presented at the American Psychological Society's annual conference (May 2017).

Engineering Plastic-Digesting Bacteria

I was part of Yale's iGEM (international Genomically Engineered Machinery) team in the summer of 2017. Our goal was to genetically E. coli capable of digesting polyethylene terephthalate (PET) plastics efficiently in a landfill situation. To do this, multiple non-native pathways including a PETase and MHETase pathway were engineered into the bacteria. The bacteria then underwent adaptive evolution and multiplex autonomic genomic engineering (MAGE) to increase the rate of plastic metabolism. We will compete later in the International Jamboree this year (Summer 2017).
